What I Do

🧠 Neural Interfaces

At Meta, I work on the neural wristband project — developing non-invasive brain-computer interfaces that decode neural signals from your wrist to enable intuitive control of AR/VR devices. It's the future of human-computer interaction.

🔬 Research Background

My neuroscience research focused on motor control and sensorimotor integration. I studied how the brain controls movement, particularly in rodent whisking behavior, using computational models and electrophysiology.

🤖 Machine Learning

I apply ML techniques to decode neural signals, understand motor patterns, and build predictive models of brain activity. The intersection of neuroscience and AI is where the most exciting breakthroughs happen.

🕹️ Time Capsule

Back in 2005, I built a custom arcade machine from scratch — complete with classic games and wonderfully dated web design. It's a fun blast from the past that captures the spirit of DIY maker culture before it was cool.
